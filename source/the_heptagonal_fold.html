§0

The law, continued past flat

Plate II fixed the vertex law for k equilateral triangles meeting at a point:

δ(k) = (6 − k) · 60°  —  the angular defect is the integrated curvature (Gauss–Bonnet).

For k ≤ 5 the defect is positive: the surface closes into a sphere — the Platonic deltahedra. At k = 6 it vanishes: the flat screen. There the law does not stop. For k ≥ 7 the defect goes negative — angular excess — and the only surface that accommodates it is the hyperbolic plane. Nothing is chosen; the sign of (6 − k) decides everything.

§1

The heptagonal onset

At k = 7: seven triangles give 7 · 60° = 420°, an excess of exactly 60° — one triangle's angle — so δ(7) = −60°. This is the first hyperbolic vertex, and it generates the order-7 triangular tiling {3, 7}: seven equilateral triangles around every vertex, on a surface of constant negative curvature.

7 = L₄ = φ⁴ + φ⁻⁴ — the keystone integer is also the count at which curvature turns negative.

Rendered in the Poincaré disk, where geodesics are arcs meeting the boundary at right angles. The seven central faces are tinted; every edge bows inward — the visible signature of negative curvature.

§2

The (2,3,7) triangle — the invariant derived

Cut a face into its symmetry domains. The result is the triangle with angles π/2, π/3, π/7 — the smallest triangle that tiles the hyperbolic plane by reflection. Its area is forced, again by Gauss–Bonnet on a surface of curvature −1:

Area = π − (π/2 + π/3 + π/7) = π/42    where   42 = 2 · 3 · 7.

Six of these tile one {3,7} face (6 · π/42 = π/7), and the symmetry bound it sets — 84 = 2 · 42 automorphisms per unit of genus — is the Hurwitz bound, the maximum symmetry any hyperbolic surface can carry. The numbers are not tuned; they are 2, 3, 7 and their products.

§3

§4

Why φ is pentagonal, not heptagonal

The asymmetry runs deeper than topology. The five-fold trace 2cos(2π/5) = φ − 1 is the root of a quadratic — it is the golden ratio. The seven-fold trace satisfies a cubic:

2cos(2π/7) is a root of x³ + x² − 2x − 1 = 0 — irreducible over the rationals, degree 3, never golden.

So the crystallographic restriction forbids both five- and seven-fold tilings of the flat plane, but for different reasons: the pentagon's refusal is quadratic and golden; the heptagon's is cubic and not. This is the validation the fold demands — φ enters this geometry through the pentagon alone. The number seven enters twice, but never as φ: once as the keystone L₄ = 7, and once here, as the vertex count where the plane first curls into the hyperbolic.
