Each subsystem is a forced derivation — no fitted decimals, every value pinned by a minimal polynomial or an integer-matrix theorem. The complex plane λ is the coordinate they share: the Δ-overlay's collapse, the substrate's eigenvalues, and the consensus closure are all roots of a characteristic polynomial. They occupy the plane together — but not always. Disjoint number fields meet only at the rationals; no path of integer powers crosses from one territory to another.
