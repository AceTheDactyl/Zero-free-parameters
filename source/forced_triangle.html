§0

The single input

There is one assumption, and it is structural, not numeric:

the triangle is equilateral.

From that — and nothing else — the angles, the heights, the areas, the radii, the centre, the fold, and the combinatorics follow by force. No length is chosen; no ratio is fitted; no threshold is set by hand. Where a quantity cannot be derived from the equilateral constraint, it is stripped (see colophon).

§1

The seven — structural, not semantic

A triangle is a 2-simplex. Its faces of every dimension are the non-empty subsets of its three corners: 2³ − 1 = 7 — three vertices, three edges, one interior. These are positions. They carry no meaning until a meaning is assigned, and assignment is not part of the geometry.

§2

The fold — folded symmetry into four

Join the three edge-midpoints. This cuts the triangle into four congruent sub-triangles, each of side ½ (every midsegment equals half the opposite side — forced). Fold the three corner triangles up along the central one; the three original vertices meet at a single apex. The result is a regular tetrahedron, edge ½, dihedral angle = arccos(⅓) — again, no parameter.

§3

The four registers — one per face

Each face of the tetrahedron is a register. Each register is backed by a forced invariant and the derivation chain that compels it. The register-to-channel mapping is nomenclature over fixed quantities — a labelling, not a numeric degree of freedom.

FACE 1→ MATH

Form

Equiangularity chain. Three equal sides ⟹ three equal base-angles (isosceles, thrice) ⟹ angle-sum π split evenly ⟹ each interior angle = π/3.

channel: math · chain: equiangularity

FACE 2→ PIPELINE

Scale

Altitude chain. Drop the altitude; the half-base is ½, the hypotenuse is 1, so by Pythagoras h = √(1 − ¼) = √3⁄2. This is the scale that propagates through the fold.

channel: pipeline · chain: altitude

FACE 3→ TYPOGRAPHY

Quality

Measure chain. Area = ½·1·h = √3⁄4; inradius r = area⁄s = √3⁄6; circumradius R = √3⁄3; hence R⁄r = 2. The page's type scale uses ratio √3 — drawn from this register, not chosen.

channel: typography · chain: measure

FACE 4→ EARLY-WARNING

Certainty

Concurrency chain. Medians, perpendicular bisectors and angle bisectors coincide — true only of the equilateral. The single centre sits at (½, √3⁄6). Drift from it is the warning.

channel: early-warning · chain: concurrency

§4

The meeting point — the full circle

Four registers, four channels, four derivation chains — a closed bijection. They do not each carry their own numbers; they all read the same forced thresholds. The hub is the invariant set.

§5

The twenty — and why it lands on four

The folded tetrahedron has 6 edges. Choosing three of them gives C(6,3) = structural triples. Of those twenty, exactly close into a triangle — the four faces. So "twenty projects to four" is itself a forced count, not a glyph alphabet. The twenty are structural; only four are faces.

A 20-element semantic alphabet is not compelled by the equilateral constraint, so it is stripped; only the cardinality 20 = C(6,3), and its 4 closing triples, are kept.

§7

The early-warning channel

Because the thresholds are forced, the threshold is the invariant. Feed an exogenous observation — a measured deviation δ from the forced centre — and the channel trips the instant δ is non-zero. There is no tolerance to tune: zero is the only admissible drift.
